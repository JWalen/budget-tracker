## Transactions Management

Transactions are the core of your budget - every penny in or out.

### Adding Transactions

#### Quick Add (Top of page)
1. Choose **Income** or **Expense** tab
2. Enter amount
3. Select category
4. Add description (optional but recommended)
5. Choose date (defaults to today)
6. Click "Add Transaction"

#### What Makes a Good Description?
- Be specific: "Grocery shopping at Walmart" not just "Shopping"
- Include vendor: "Shell Gas Station on Main St"
- Add context: "Birthday dinner with friends"
- This helps with searching and remembering later

### Managing Existing Transactions

#### Filtering & Searching
- **By Type** - Show only income or expenses
- **By Category** - Filter to specific categories
- **By Date** - Use the date range picker
- **Search** - Find transactions by description

#### Bulk Actions
- Select multiple transactions using checkboxes
- Delete multiple at once
- Export selected transactions

### Recurring Transactions

**When to use:** For automatic, fixed-amount transactions that happen on a schedule.

#### Perfect For:
- ✅ **Salary/wages** - Automatically record your paycheck
- ✅ **Subscriptions** - Netflix, Spotify, gym membership
- ✅ **Fixed bills** - Insurance, loan payments that never change
- ✅ **Savings transfers** - Weekly/monthly savings deposits

#### How to Set Up:
1. Go to Transactions → Recurring tab
2. Click "Add Recurring"
3. Enter details:
   - **Amount** - Must be the same every time
   - **Description** - What this payment is for
   - **Category** - Which budget category it belongs to
   - **Frequency** - Daily, weekly, biweekly, monthly, or yearly
   - **Next Date** - When should it start?

#### Important Notes:
- Recurring transactions create entries **automatically**
- They appear in your transaction history immediately
- They affect your balance and reports
- Edit the recurring rule to change future transactions
- Past transactions remain unchanged

## Categories System

Categories are how you organize and understand your spending.

### Default Categories
The system starts with common categories, but you should customize them:

## Bills vs Recurring Transactions

This is a common confusion point - here's the complete breakdown:

### Bills (Bills Page)

**Purpose:** Track and manage payments that need your attention

#### Use Bills When:
- 📋 **Amount varies** - Electricity, water, credit cards
- 📋 **Manual payment** - You write a check or initiate transfer
- 📋 **Need reminders** - Track what's paid and what's due
- 📋 **Want to assign to paychecks** - Pay rent with 1st check, utilities with 2nd
- 📋 **Track payment status** - Know what's paid/unpaid/overdue at a glance

#### How Bills Work:
1. **Set up the bill** - Name, typical amount, due date
2. **Each month** - Bill appears as "unpaid"
3. **When you pay** - Mark as paid (creates transaction or links to existing)
4. **Status tracking** - Green (paid), Yellow (due soon), Red (overdue)

#### Marking Bills as Paid:
Two options when marking paid:
1. **Link Transaction** - You already recorded the payment, just link them
2. **Create Expense** - Automatically create the transaction record

### Recurring Transactions (Transactions → Recurring)

**Purpose:** Automatically create transactions for fixed, regular payments

#### Use Recurring When:
- 🔄 **Same amount always** - $1,500 rent, $50 internet
- 🔄 **Automatic payment** - Auto-debit from your account
- 🔄 **Income** - Salary, pension, regular deposits
- 🔄 **Set and forget** - Don't want to think about it monthly

#### How Recurring Works:
1. **Set up once** - Amount, frequency, start date
2. **Runs automatically** - Creates transactions on schedule
3. **No interaction needed** - Just happens in background
4. **Always affects balance** - Immediately impacts your numbers

### Can I Use Both?

**Yes!** Example scenario:
- **Electric Bill**: Set up as Bill (varies $80-150, due on 15th)
- **Netflix**: Set up as Recurring Transaction ($15.99 auto-debit on 5th)
- **Rent**: Could be both:
  - Bill to track due date and ensure it's paid
  - Recurring if it's auto-paid from your account

## Pay Periods & Bill Assignment

Pay Periods help you plan which bills to pay with which paycheck - essential for paycheck-to-paycheck budgeting.

### What Are Pay Periods?

Think of them as "income buckets" - each paycheck is a bucket, and you assign bills to each bucket.

### Setting Up Pay Periods

1. **Navigate** to Pay Periods page
2. **Add your income sources**:
   - Name: "Main Job Paycheck" or "Freelance Payment"
   - Amount: Your take-home pay
   - Date: When you receive it
   - Recurring: Toggle on for regular paychecks

#### Recurring Pay Periods:
- **Weekly** - Every Friday
- **Biweekly** - Every other Friday
- **Semi-monthly** - 1st and 15th
- **Monthly** - Same day each month

### Assigning Bills to Pay Periods

This is the magic - telling the system which bills get paid from which paycheck.

#### How to Assign:
1. **On Pay Periods page** - Click "+ Assign Bill" on any pay period
2. **Select the bill** from dropdown
3. **System tracks** remaining money after each bill

#### Smart Assignment Strategy:
- **1st paycheck**: Rent/mortgage, car payment (big fixed bills)
- **2nd paycheck**: Utilities, credit cards (variable bills)
- **Spread evenly**: Don't overload one paycheck

#### What You'll See:
- **Total Income**: The paycheck amount
- **Total Assigned**: Bills assigned to this check
- **Remaining**: What's left for other expenses
- **Color coding**: Green (money left), Red (overassigned)

### Why Use Pay Periods?

- **Never miss a bill** - See what needs paying from each check
- **Avoid overdrafts** - Know if a paycheck is overloaded
- **Plan ahead** - See future pay periods and obligations
- **Peace of mind** - Everything is accounted for

## Budgets & Spending Limits

Budgets are spending limits you set for each category - your financial guardrails.

### Creating Budgets

1. **Go to** Budgets page
2. **Select** month and year
3. **For each category**, set a limit:
   - Look at past spending for guidance
   - Start with necessities (housing, food, transport)
   - Then add discretionary (entertainment, shopping)

### Budget Strategies

#### 50/30/20 Rule:
- **50%** - Needs (housing, utilities, food)
- **30%** - Wants (entertainment, dining out)
- **20%** - Savings and debt payment

#### Zero-Based Budget:
- Every dollar has a job
- Income minus all budgets = 0
- Forces intentional spending

#### Pay Yourself First:
- Set savings budget first
- Then necessities
- Whatever's left is discretionary

### Monitoring Budget Progress

#### On Dashboard:
- Progress bars show spending vs budget
- Colors indicate status (green/yellow/red)
- Percentage shows how much is used

#### On Budgets Page:
- Detailed view of each category
- Actual transactions in that category
- Days left in month
- Suggested daily spending to stay on track

### When You're Over Budget

Options when you exceed a limit:
1. **Adjust the budget** - If it was unrealistic
2. **Reduce spending** - Stop that category for the month
3. **Transfer budget** - Reduce another category to compensate
4. **Accept it** - Sometimes life happens, plan better next month

## Debts & Loans Tracking

Track what you owe and to whom - critical for financial health.

### Types of Debt to Track

#### Money You Owe:
- **Credit cards** - Track balances and minimum payments
- **Loans** - Personal, auto, student loans
- **Mortgage** - Your home loan
- **Personal debts** - Money borrowed from family/friends

#### Money Owed to You:
- **Personal loans** - Money you've lent out
- **Business receivables** - If you freelance/contract
- **Reimbursements** - Waiting for expense repayments

### Setting Up Debts

Required Information:
- **Name** - "Chase Visa" or "Car Loan - Toyota"
- **Type** - Owe/Owed/Loan/Credit Card
- **Current Balance** - What's owed right now
- **Original Amount** - Starting balance (for progress tracking)
- **Interest Rate** - For calculating true cost
- **Minimum Payment** - Monthly obligation
- **Due Date** - Day of month payment is due

### Debt Strategies

#### Avalanche Method:
1. Pay minimums on all debts
2. Extra money goes to highest interest rate
3. Saves the most money long-term

#### Snowball Method:
1. Pay minimums on all debts
2. Extra money goes to smallest balance
3. Quick wins for motivation

#### Tracking Progress:
- See total debt amount
- Track payoff progress
- Calculate interest costs
- Estimate payoff dates

### Sharing Levels

- Can see all your financial data
- Cannot make changes

## Bank Import Feature

Import transactions from your bank - save time on data entry.

### Supported Formats

- **CSV** - Most common, works with all banks
- **QFX/OFX** - Quicken format
- **QIF** - Older Quicken format

### How to Export from Your Bank

#### Most Banks:
1. Log into online banking
2. Find "Download" or "Export" option
3. Select date range
4. Choose CSV format
5. Save file

### Importing Process

1. **Go to** Bank Import page
2. **Upload** your file
3. **Map columns** - Tell system which column is what:
   - Date
   - Description
   - Amount (or separate Debit/Credit)
4. **Review** transactions
5. **Auto-match** to existing bills/categories
6. **Manually categorize** remaining
7. **Import** to add to your budget

### Import Tips

#### Before Importing:
- Set up your categories first
- Create your bills
- Set up match rules for common transactions

#### Duplicate Prevention:
- System checks for duplicates
- Compares date and amount
- Warns before importing

#### Match Rules:
Create rules like:
- "NETFLIX.COM" → Entertainment category
- "EMPLOYER DEPOSIT" → Salary category
- "MORTGAGE PAYMENT" → Housing category

## Tips & Best Practices

### Daily Habits (2 minutes)
- ✅ Add any cash expenses immediately
- ✅ Check your dashboard summary
- ✅ Review any overdue bills

### Weekly Routine (10 minutes)
- ✅ Review and categorize imported transactions
- ✅ Check budget progress
- ✅ Mark bills as paid
- ✅ Reconcile with bank balance

### Monthly Process (30 minutes)
1. **Import** bank transactions
2. **Review** all transactions for accuracy
3. **Analyze** reports for insights
4. **Adjust** next month's budgets
5. **Set up** new bills or recurring transactions
6. **Check** debt progress
7. **Plan** for upcoming expenses

### Getting Maximum Value

#### Start Simple:
- Don't try to use every feature at once
- Master transactions and categories first
- Add complexity as you get comfortable

#### Be Honest:
- Track everything, even embarrassing purchases
- Use real numbers, not optimistic ones
- Include cash spending

#### Stay Consistent:
- Same categories each time
- Regular review schedule
- Don't skip months

#### Use Descriptions:
- Future you will thank present you
- Makes searching easier
- Helps at tax time

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Too many categories** - Impossible to manage
✅ Keep it under 15-20

❌ **Forgetting cash** - Leads to "missing" money
✅ Track cash expenses immediately

❌ **Unrealistic budgets** - Setting yourself up to fail
✅ Base on actual past spending

❌ **Ignoring it** - Not checking for weeks
✅ Quick daily check, weekly review

❌ **Not tracking small expenses** - They add up!
✅ Everything counts, even $1 coffee

### Advanced Strategies

#### Envelope Method (Digital):
- Create a category for each "envelope"
- Set strict budgets
- When it's gone, it's gone

#### Sinking Funds:
- Create categories for future expenses
- "Christmas", "Vacation", "Car Maintenance"
- Add money monthly
- Spend when needed

#### Emergency Fund Tracking:
- Create "Emergency Fund" as a category
- Set a goal amount as budget
- Track progress monthly

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### "My balance doesn't match my bank"
- Check for missed transactions
- Import latest bank data
- Look for pending transactions

#### "Bills show as overdue but I paid them"
- Mark as paid in the Bills page
- Link to the payment transaction

#### "Budget shows overspent but I have money"
- Budgets are per category, not total
- Check if money is in other categories

#### "Can't delete a category"
- Must have no transactions
- Move transactions to another category first

#### "Import creates duplicates"
- Check date ranges
- System prevents exact duplicates
- Manual review before importing
